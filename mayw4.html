Meta Ad Policy -- Safe But Strong
Mandatory

Every headline must be writeable on Meta without getting rejected. Safe does not mean bland -- the reference headlines below show the voice we want to match. Strong, abstract, observational. Never claim-led, never personal-attribute, never named brand.

Do NOT use
  • Named hotel brands (Four Seasons, Ritz-Carlton, Westin, Marriott, Hyatt, etc) -- third-party trademarks plus implied endorsement
  • Named competitor pillow or bedding brands of any kind
  • Personal attributes -- "your neck pain," "your snoring," "you are exhausted," "your back hurts" -- direct call-out flags Meta review
  • Medical claims -- "cures," "ends," "fixes," "stops" any condition
  • Diagnostic language -- "if you have X, you need Y" framings
  • Before/after / negative health framing -- "are you tired all the time?" "do you wake up sore?"
  • Unsubstantiated superlatives -- "the best pillow in the world," "guaranteed best sleep"
  • Specialist endorsement claims -- "chiropractors recommend," "doctors recommend" -- unless we can substantiate with documentation
DO use
  • Generic category references -- "luxury hotels," "5-star hotels," "the world's best hotels," "hotel-grade fill"
  • Observational language -- "mornings can feel different," "sleep specialists talk about the pillow first"
  • Abstract/aspirational framing -- the 12 reference headlines below set the voice
  • Product specifications -- "down alternative," "hotel-grade fill," "hypoallergenic," "RDS certified"
  • Factual social proof -- "794,000 sleepers," "5-star reviews," "100-night trial"
  • Price claims -- $69.90 is a documented price, safe to use
  • Universal experience -- "the way mornings feel," "the way couples sleep" -- not directed AT the viewer
Rule of thumb: if a headline calls OUT the viewer ("you are X," "your X is broken"), it is unsafe. If it describes a universal observation ("mornings feel different," "the pillow is the variable"), it is safe.

Standing Instruction for Blitzads

Do not produce 500 assets as visual templates with swapped copy. Section 1 must feel meaningfully distinct from MAY W3 -- not a continuation by volume, but by audience cut and creative entry-point. Within a track, no two assets should be variations of each other.

Section 1 reminders: Track 1 (Unaware) carries 7 audience cuts -- each cut should feel visually anchored to its life stage. Track 2 stays travel-coded. Track 3 stays wellness-coded. Track 4 uses retargeting-voice copy, not introduction-voice copy.

Section 2 URL discipline: Each LP asset must drive to its own dedicated URL. Do not point chiropractor-angle assets at the gift LP. Blitzads to QC URL-asset matching before delivery.

If a Section 2 LP card asset could swap places with an asset from a different LP without anyone noticing, it is not distinct enough. Regenerate.

Meta-safety is non-negotiable. If a headline names a hotel brand, calls out a viewer's body or condition, makes a medical claim, or could be flagged as a personal-attribute attack, it does not ship. The Meta Ad Policy block above and the 12 reference headlines define the safe-but-strong target.
